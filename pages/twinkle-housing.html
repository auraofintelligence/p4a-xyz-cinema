Twinkle 05 / Housing

Rent went up. He painted one wall.

A renter-maths rant about paying more every year for the same walls, then a hard turn: there is no single magic fix, you model every housing solution in public and run the mix that works.

The everyday gripe

Straight to camera: rents have outrun wages for years, vacancy is near zero, and the annual increase letter arrives with a fresh coat of paint on exactly one wall. The joke is renter maths. The trapdoor is a public workbench where every housing fix gets modelled in the open, no single silver bullet, so the solutions that actually work can run together. This seed is open: film your own version, your suburb, your numbers.

Description

BaitRent is the one bill a third of the country feels every single week, and it has been outrunning pay for half a decade.
SwitchOut of the landlord-versus-renter brawl and into the room where ownership models, co-ops, public stock and stewardship get tested side by side.
SystemHousing simulations: model every fix in public, keep the assumptions visible, and run the mix that works. Not one magic policy, a whole portfolio tested in daylight.

The one-breath drop

Said before the flush

"There is no magic pill: you model every housing fix in the open and run the mix that works." It's a spreadsheet, not a class war, and the second breath lives in the room.
