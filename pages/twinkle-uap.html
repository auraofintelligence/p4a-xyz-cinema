UFO hearings in America. Crickets here.

A backyard-telescope rant: the Americans hold UFO hearings on live TV, our officials won't even say the word. Then a hard turn into a public channel where weird claims get checked instead of laughed off.

The everyday gripe

Straight to camera, esky lid for a lectern: the Americans hold congressional hearings, release the cockpit footage and brief their parliament. Here, the official answer is a polite cough. Whatever is or isn't up there, the gripe is the silence: adults can handle "we don't know". The trapdoor is method for the weird: a standing public channel where a mystery goes in, the evidence gets graded, corrections are public, and nobody pays the giggle tax for asking. This seed is open: film your own "just tell us" rant.

Description

BaitUAP talk is already in every pub, and half the country has watched the released footage. Official silence is the only part that still feels strange.
SwitchFrom "what are they hiding" to "why is there no method": secrecy-by-default is a habit, and it costs trust on every topic, not just this one.
SystemThe truth engine: claim labels, evidence grades, correction paths and public audit trails. Machinery that doesn't flinch at weird is machinery you can trust with everything else.
