Twinkle 10 / Beer tax

Beer tax gets a payrise. You don't.

A bar-stool rant about the one government process that runs like a Swiss watch, then a hard turn into why a tax can be automatic but donation disclosure somehow takes a year.

Open the seedFollow-through: Civic ledger

Open seed

The everyday gripe

Straight to camera, schooner in shot: the beer tax goes up every February and every August, automatically, no debate, no delay, among the highest beer taxes going. Meanwhile the money that funds political campaigns surfaces long after the votes are counted. The gag writes itself: this country CAN run things like clockwork, it just chooses which things. This seed is open: film your own price-of-a-schooner history lesson.

Description

BaitThe pub test held in an actual pub. Everyone who buys a beer watches indexation happen twice a year with total reliability.
SwitchThe gripe is not really the beer. It is that automation and punctuality only ever seem to point one direction: at you.
SystemThe civic ledger: taxes, donations and promises on the same public rail, updating as reliably as the beer tax does.

The one-breath drop

Said before the flush

"If the beer tax can raise itself twice a year automatically, donations can disclose themselves the same day. Same computer." Second breath lives in the room.
