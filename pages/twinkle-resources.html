Twinkle 13 / Local resources

They shipped the beach to the city.

A jetty-end rant about sand, gravel, water and timber that leave a place forever while the potholes stay, then a hard turn into every load landing on that place's own public ledger.

Open seed

The everyday gripe

Straight to camera: whole suburbs of the capital are standing on sand that used to be somebody's island, and the island got a wave and a rehab plan. Every extractive town knows the shape: the resource leaves, the royalty evaporates somewhere upstream, and local memory of who took what fades in a filing cabinet. The trapdoor is a ledger that belongs to the place: every load out, every dollar back, on the record forever. This seed is open: film what left your town.

Description

BaitEvery regional and coastal community has a version of this story, told with a point of the finger: that hill, that quarry, that bore, that beach.
SwitchFrom nostalgia to arithmetic: extraction is not the crime, amnesia is. Who counted the loads, and where can anyone read the count?
SystemPlace-based public ledgers: the Minjerribah room shows island-scale thinking, and Public Assets holds the long-memory pattern for every deal that touches a place.

The one-breath drop

Said before the flush

"Every truckload that leaves a place goes on that place's public ledger, royalties attached, forever." Second breath lives in the room.
