Not a merch brand. A ladder.

Every other store wants your money and hopes you never think about where it went. This one shows you the cost, lets you choose the support on top, and publishes where every dollar landed. Gear funds tables, tables earn trust, trust builds a party. That's the whole trick, in daylight.

Regional starter kits

Source it in your own town first.

These kits are shopping lists with honest ranges, not boxes we ship. Local hardware, local op-shops, local printers: the money stays where the table stands. One rule shapes the list: never buy what a crew already carries in duplicate. Three or four people at a table have cameras, mics and editors in their pockets, so the kits fund what pockets don't hold: AI seats, a kiosk that survives an outage, and data on your own shelf. Where a real quote is needed, it says so.

Templates named in the kits (run sheets, consent notes, issue cards, handover packs) live free in the starter field kit and the repo. Kits change with region and season: quotes beat estimates, and the ledger beats both.

Candidates, not a catalogue

Tell us what you'd actually wear.

One test for every line: would you wear or carry it if the politics were stripped off? If it only works because of the logo, it's landfill with a cause printed on it. That rule already deleted the lanyards, the tea towel and the branded notebook. What's left is a starting guess, and it's allowed to be wrong: what gets quoted is what people ask for, not what we imagined at a keyboard.

Requests so far

Nobody has asked for anything yet.

Because nobody has been asked yet. That's the honest number, and it beats a catalogue nobody wanted. Tell us what you'd wear, what colour purple is the right purple, and what you'd never be seen in. Requests drive the quote queue: an item gets a supplier when people want it, not before.

Who we ask, in order

Sovereign makers first. Print mills last.

This ladder is the difference between a party store and a Vistaprint account. Rank five exists so the rule can be broken honestly and out loud, rather than quietly.

On rank two: we ask and invite, and businesses onboard themselves on their own terms. We list nobody without consent and speak for nobody. Country and community come before our print run, every time.

The process

How a new supplier gets added.

The process is the product. Anyone should be able to add a supplier without asking Luke, and anyone should be able to check the work afterwards. Six gated steps, so a supplier cannot reach the store before it has been sampled and dated.

The one unforgivable move

Never touch the date to make the badge go green.

The store ages its own prices: after 90 days a number flags itself stale whether or not anyone remembered. That automation exists because the failure mode of an honest store is not lying, it is forgetting, and a figure left sitting for two years quietly becomes false. When it flags, re-quote it or mark it reference. Editing the date to silence the badge is the exact move the rest of this site exists to argue against.

Open books

Where the money goes, before you're asked for any.

Every order splits four ways in the open: the garment's base cost, the print, the payment fee, and the support amount you chose yourself, which can be zero. The order sheet already records every row; the next step, described here before it is built, is a public ledger page fed straight from those rows: totals in, costs out, activities funded, receipts linked. Today's honest number is zero sales and zero dollars, and we'd rather publish that than a vibe.
