Regional starter kits

Source it in your own town first.

Shopping lists with honest ranges, not boxes we ship. One rule shapes them: never buy what a crew already carries in duplicate. Pockets hold the cameras; the kits fund what pockets don't.

Templates named in the kits live free in the starter field kit and the repo. Quotes beat estimates, and the ledger beats both.

Who we ask, in order

Sovereign makers first. Print mills last.

The ladder fades as it descends: bright is local and sovereign, grey is the generic mill. Rank five exists so the rule can only ever be broken out loud.

On rank two: we ask and invite, and businesses onboard themselves on their own terms. We list nobody without consent and speak for nobody. Country and community come before our print run, every time.

The process

How a new supplier gets added.

Six gated steps, so a supplier cannot reach the store before it has been sampled and dated. Anyone can run it without asking Luke; anyone can check the work afterwards.

The one unforgivable move

Never touch the date to make the badge go green.

The store ages its own prices: after 90 days a number flags itself stale whether or not anyone remembered. When it flags, re-quote it or mark it reference. Editing the date to silence the badge is the exact move the rest of this site exists to argue against.
